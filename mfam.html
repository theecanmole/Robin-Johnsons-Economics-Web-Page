Robin Johnson's family histories

  • Thomas Johnson: A Pioneer and Seer is a record of Robin's grandfather's migration to and life in New Zealand from 1863. This article appeared in The Family Historian, in 2006.
  • The Life of Hannah Johnson A Memoire is a brief record of the life of Robin's father's sister, born in 1885 in Wellington.
  • Love at first tooth: family and missionary politics in East Africa and Congo 1913-1934 is the life story of Elizabeth Ann Thompson and Ruben Bishop Flinn, parents of Robin's wife, Ruth Flinn, and sometime missionaries in the Congo and Tanganyika in the period 1913-1934.
  • Dorothy E Johnson; A Memoir (1993) is a personal history of Robin's mother, Dorothy Elfreda Johnson, born in 1891, died 1980. It was contributed to a book about Quakers in New Zealand, Seeking a New Land: Quakers in New Zealand, edited by James and Audrey Brodie, The Peachtree Press, Wellington, 1993.
  • Robin's Obituary. Rod Forbes started a draft and I wrote the rest. I had arranged with a journalist from the Dominion Post for the obituary to appear in late September 2008. However, the obituary for the Pink Floyd keyboard player Rick White appeared instead.
  • Life Membership of New Zealand Agricultural Economics Society (NZAES). In 1996, Robin was made the first life member of the New Zealand Agricultural Economics Society (NZAES). This is the recommendation, moved by Ram SriRamaratnam, the 1996 President.
  • Working with the YMCA during World War One, The New Zealand Genealogist, Vol 39, No 313, pages 313-318, September/October 2008. Robin and I sent this paper off to The New Zealand Genealogist in June 2008 and it was published in October.
